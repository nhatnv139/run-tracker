## 3. Consult Your Physician Before Starting

You should consult a physician or qualified healthcare professional **before starting** any training program, particularly if you:

- Have a history of or are being treated for cardiovascular disease, hypertension, or stroke;
- Have respiratory conditions (asthma, COPD);
- Have diabetes, kidney disease, or liver disease;
- Have musculoskeletal injuries, herniated discs, or arthritis;
- Are pregnant, recently gave birth, or are breastfeeding;
- Are recovering from surgery or serious illness;
- Are over 60 years old and not accustomed to exercise;
- Have an eating disorder or are taking medication that affects heart rate, blood pressure, or hydration.

Ask your physician about appropriate intensity, target heart rate zones, and caloric needs given your health condition.

## 4. Stop-Exercise Warnings

**Stop exercising immediately** and seek medical help if you experience:

- Chest pain, shortness of breath, or abnormal palpitations;
- Dizziness, lightheadedness, or loss of balance;
- Nausea, vomiting, or excessive cold sweat;
- Sharp pain, swelling, or loss of function in any joint;
- Sudden vision loss, slurred speech, or numbness/weakness on one side of the body.

Call your local emergency number (**112** in EU, **911** in US, **115** in Vietnam, **999** in UK).

## 5. AI Coach Cautions

5.1. RunVie's AI Coach uses Anthropic's Claude model to provide personalised training suggestions based on your workout data, goals, and information you supply.

5.2. **Limitations of AI Coach:**
- It may produce inaccurate or incomplete information (hallucination).
- It does not replace a sports physician, certified personal trainer, or qualified nutritionist.
- It cannot access your medical records and is unaware of your full medical history.
- It cannot interpret medical imaging, lab results, or ECGs.

5.3. **Never** use AI Coach to diagnose symptoms, prescribe medication, or make decisions about acute medical conditions. In emergencies, contact emergency services directly.

## 6. Outdoor Training Safety

6.1. When running or walking outdoors, you must:
- Comply with applicable traffic and road-use laws;
- Use pedestrian paths and crossings;
- Wear reflective gear during low-light conditions;
- Inform a family member or friend of your route and expected return time;
- Check weather conditions; avoid extreme heat (heat index > 40 °C / 104 °F), storms, or heavy rain.

6.2. RunVie does not control or verify the safety of routes recommended by the App or shared by other users. **You assess and accept the risk** before using any route.

## 7. Sensor Accuracy

7.1. Heart rate, steps, calories, elevation, and speed are estimated from mobile device or wearable sensors and **may not be accurate** compared to medically calibrated equipment.

7.2. Margin of error is particularly high under:
- Weak GPS signal (indoors, tunnels, dense tree cover);
- Loose or excessively tight wrist contact;
- Water or extreme temperatures;
- Low battery levels.

7.3. Do not rely on App data to make medical decisions or as a substitute for medical instruments (blood pressure cuffs, ECG monitors, glucometers, etc.).
