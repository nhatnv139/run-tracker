# DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT (DPA)

**RunVie Corporate Wellness Programme**

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective date:** 20 May 2026

This Data Processing Agreement (the "DPA") forms part of the Master Services Agreement, Order Form, or other written or electronic agreement (the "Principal Agreement") between:

- **RunVie Company Limited**, a company incorporated under the laws of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam, with registered office at [Registered office address] (hereinafter the "**Processor**" or "**RunVie**"); and
- **[Client Legal Name]**, with registered office at [Client address] (hereinafter the "**Controller**" or "**Client**").

Each a "Party", collectively the "Parties".

---

## Recitals

(A) The Controller has engaged the Processor to provide the RunVie Corporate Wellness service ("**Services**"), under which the Processor processes personal data of the Controller's employees, contractors, and authorised programme participants ("**Data Subjects**") on behalf of the Controller.

(B) This DPA establishes the data protection obligations of the Parties pursuant to Article 28 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (the "**GDPR**"), Decree No. 13/2023/ND-CP on Personal Data Protection of Vietnam, the UK Data Protection Act 2018, and where applicable the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA).

(C) The Parties agree as follows.

---

## 1. Definitions

Capitalised terms not defined here have the meaning given in the GDPR or the Principal Agreement.

- **Applicable Data Protection Laws** means GDPR, UK GDPR, Decree 13/2023/ND-CP, CCPA/CPRA and any other privacy laws applicable to the Processing.
- **Personal Data** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by the Processor on behalf of the Controller under the Principal Agreement.
- **Processing** has the meaning given in Art. 4(2) GDPR.
- **Sub-processor** means any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
- **Data Subject Request** means a request from a Data Subject to exercise their rights under Applicable Data Protection Laws.
- **Personal Data Breach** has the meaning given in Art. 4(12) GDPR.
- **Annex I, II, III, IV** are the schedules attached to this DPA describing details of Processing, Technical and Organisational Measures, approved Sub-processors, and Standard Contractual Clauses, respectively.

## 2. Subject Matter, Duration, Nature and Purpose

2.1. **Subject matter.** Processing of Personal Data of the Controller's employees and participants for the purposes of corporate wellness, fitness tracking, employee engagement analytics (aggregate), and reward programmes.

2.2. **Duration.** The DPA is in force for the duration of the Principal Agreement and any agreed post-termination period required for data return or deletion.

2.3. **Nature of Processing.** Storage, organisation, analysis (aggregate dashboards), display, transmission, and deletion.

2.4. **Purpose.** As set out in Annex I.

2.5. **Categories of Data Subjects and Personal Data.** As set out in Annex I.

## 3. Roles and Compliance

3.1. The Controller is the controller of Personal Data and the Processor processes Personal Data on its behalf.

3.2. Each Party shall comply with its respective obligations under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

3.3. The Controller warrants that it has a valid legal basis and, where required, has obtained the necessary consents from Data Subjects for the Processor to process Personal Data under this DPA.

## 4. Processor Obligations

The Processor shall:

4.1. Process Personal Data only on **documented instructions** from the Controller, including with regard to international transfers, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law to which the Processor is subject (in which case the Processor shall inform the Controller, unless prohibited by law).

4.2. Ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data have committed themselves to **confidentiality** or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

4.3. Implement appropriate **technical and organisational measures** as set out in Annex II to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk (Art. 32 GDPR).

4.4. Respect the conditions in Sections 5 and 6 for engaging Sub-processors.

4.5. **Assist the Controller**, taking into account the nature of the Processing and the information available to the Processor:
  (a) in fulfilling Data Subject Requests (Arts. 15–22 GDPR);
  (b) in ensuring compliance with obligations pursuant to Arts. 32–36 GDPR (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation);
  (c) in meeting its obligations under Decree 13/2023/ND-CP (impact assessment dossier, cross-border transfer dossier).

4.6. At the choice of the Controller, **delete or return** all Personal Data after the end of the provision of services, and delete existing copies, unless storage is required by Union, Member State or Vietnamese law.

4.7. Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in this DPA and allow for and contribute to **audits**, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller (Section 8).

4.8. **Notify** the Controller without undue delay if an instruction, in the Processor's opinion, infringes Applicable Data Protection Laws.

## 5. Sub-processing

5.1. The Controller grants the Processor general written authorisation to engage Sub-processors. The current list of approved Sub-processors is set out in **Annex III**.

5.2. The Processor shall inform the Controller of any intended addition or replacement of Sub-processors at least **30 days** in advance, giving the Controller the opportunity to object on reasonable data-protection grounds. If the Controller objects, the Parties shall discuss in good faith. If no resolution is reached, the Controller may terminate the affected Services without penalty.

5.3. Where the Processor engages a Sub-processor for carrying out specific processing activities on behalf of the Controller, the Processor shall impose on the Sub-processor, by way of a contract, the **same data protection obligations** as those set out in this DPA (Art. 28(4) GDPR).

5.4. Where the Sub-processor fails to fulfil its data protection obligations, the Processor shall remain fully liable to the Controller for the performance of that Sub-processor's obligations.

## 6. International Data Transfers

6.1. The Processor and its Sub-processors may transfer Personal Data outside the EEA, the UK, or Vietnam only where appropriate safeguards under Chapter V of the GDPR and Art. 25 of Decree 13/2023/ND-CP are in place.

6.2. Where transfers occur from the EEA to a country without an adequacy decision, the Parties hereby incorporate the **European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** of 4 June 2021, with Module Two (controller-to-processor) applying as between the Controller (data exporter) and the Processor (data importer). Selections, options and Annexes are set out in **Annex IV**.

6.3. Where transfers occur from the UK, the **UK International Data Transfer Addendum** issued by the ICO is incorporated by reference.

6.4. The Processor warrants that, prior to any cross-border transfer of Personal Data of Vietnamese Data Subjects, it shall have completed the cross-border transfer dossier as required by Art. 25 of Decree 13/2023/ND-CP.

## 7. Security, Breach Notification

7.1. The Processor shall implement and maintain the technical and organisational measures described in **Annex II**, including encryption in transit (TLS 1.3), encryption at rest (AES-256), access controls under the principle of least privilege, multi-factor authentication, audit logging, vulnerability management, and a documented incident response plan.

7.2. Upon becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach, the Processor shall:
  (a) notify the Controller **without undue delay**, and in any event within **48 hours**;
  (b) provide all information reasonably required by the Controller to comply with its obligation to notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours under Art. 33 GDPR and Art. 23 of Decree 13/2023/ND-CP;
  (c) take reasonable steps to contain, investigate, and mitigate the Breach.

7.3. The Processor shall maintain a register of Personal Data Breaches affecting Controller's Personal Data.

## 8. Audits

8.1. The Processor shall make available to the Controller, on request, the latest SOC 2 Type II report, ISO/IEC 27001 certificate, or equivalent independent third-party audit reports of the Processor or its Sub-processors.

8.2. Where reasonably required and where third-party reports are insufficient, the Controller may conduct an audit at the Processor's premises with **30 days' prior written notice**, no more than once per calendar year (except in case of a substantiated incident), at the Controller's expense, during business hours, and without disrupting operations.

8.3. The Controller and its auditors shall be bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those in the Principal Agreement.

## 9. Data Subject Requests

9.1. The Processor shall, taking into account the nature of the Processing, **assist** the Controller by appropriate technical and organisational measures in fulfilling Data Subject Requests under Arts. 15–22 GDPR and Art. 9 of Decree 13/2023/ND-CP.

9.2. The Processor shall promptly forward any Data Subject Request received directly to the Controller (within 5 business days) and shall not itself respond to such request except on the documented instruction of the Controller.

## 10. Liability and Indemnification

10.1. Liability under this DPA shall be governed by the limits and provisions set out in the Principal Agreement, subject to the rights of Data Subjects under Art. 82 GDPR which cannot be limited by contract.

10.2. Where a Data Subject obtains compensation under Art. 82 GDPR, the Parties shall, in their internal relationship, allocate liability according to their respective responsibility for the damage.

## 11. Term and Termination

11.1. This DPA terminates automatically upon termination or expiry of the Principal Agreement.

11.2. Upon termination, the Processor shall, at the Controller's option, **return or delete** all Personal Data within **60 days**, and certify deletion in writing. Backups will be overwritten in accordance with standard rolling retention schedules not exceeding 90 days.

## 12. General Provisions

12.1. **Order of precedence.** In case of conflict, the order of precedence is: (i) SCCs, (ii) this DPA, (iii) the Principal Agreement.

12.2. **Governing law.** This DPA is governed by the laws of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam, except where SCCs apply, in which case the SCCs are governed by the law of the EU Member State chosen in Annex IV.

12.3. **Severability.** If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions remain in effect.

12.4. **Signatures.** Electronic signatures are accepted under Vietnam's Law on Electronic Transactions 20/2023/QH15 and the EU eIDAS Regulation.

## Annex I – Details of Processing

- **Categories of Data Subjects:** Controller's employees, contractors, programme participants, and authorised family members.
- **Categories of Personal Data:** Name, work email, employee ID, age range, gender, height, weight (optional), workout activity data, GPS routes (optional), heart rate, steps, calories, sleep data (via HealthKit/Health Connect, with employee consent), in-app communication, device information, IP address, push token.
- **Special categories of data:** Health and fitness data (Art. 9 GDPR), processed with explicit consent.
- **Frequency of Processing:** Continuous during the term of the Services.
- **Purposes:** Wellness tracking, aggregate engagement dashboards (de-identified), corporate challenges, reward distribution.
- **Duration:** For the term of the Principal Agreement plus 60 days for data return/deletion.

## Annex II – Technical and Organisational Measures

1. **Encryption in transit:** TLS 1.3 for all data exchange.
2. **Encryption at rest:** AES-256 for databases and backups; passwords hashed with Argon2id.
3. **Access control:** Role-based access control (RBAC), least privilege, mandatory MFA for all staff with production access, just-in-time access provisioning.
4. **Network security:** Private VPC, WAF (Cloudflare), DDoS protection, segregated environments (dev/staging/prod).
5. **Logging and monitoring:** Centralised audit logs retained for 12 months; alerting via SIEM; anomaly detection.
6. **Vulnerability management:** Automated dependency scanning, quarterly internal penetration tests, annual external penetration test.
7. **Backups:** Encrypted daily backups stored in a separate region; restoration tested quarterly.
8. **Business continuity:** Documented BCP/DRP with RTO of 4 hours, RPO of 1 hour.
9. **Personnel:** Background checks; mandatory annual privacy and security training; written confidentiality undertakings.
10. **Physical security:** Hosted in Google Cloud Platform asia-southeast1 (Singapore) datacenters with ISO 27001, SOC 1/2/3 certifications.
11. **Pseudonymisation and anonymisation:** Applied for analytics dashboards visible to Controller management.
12. **Incident response:** Documented IRP; designated DPO; tabletop exercises annually.
